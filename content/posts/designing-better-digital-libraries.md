A digital library is more than a search box. Readers need a path from discovery to comprehension to reuse, especially when sources are dense or unfamiliar.

That means richer previews, clearer provenance, and citation tools that feel native to the reading flow.

## Context before commitment

People should be able to understand why a source matters before opening a long PDF or archive item. Strong previews can show abstract, date, author, collection, related themes, and source reliability.

The most effective library products reduce friction without flattening complexity. They help people inspect sources, compare context, and build confidence in what they cite.

## Design for return visits

Good library design remembers that research is recursive. Saved searches, reading lists, export formats, and citation trails make it easier for readers to resume complex work.
